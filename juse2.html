The first color I thought of when I thought of the brand name "Juse" was this orangish-red color. I thought this color would pop and grab the attention of the consumer. To compliment the orange, I decided to incorporate the teal color. From there, I incorporated neutral colors including white, gray and black to allow the two main colors to really pop.

For the logo, I wanted the style of the letters to be bubbly and grafiti-like. So I went to DaFont.com and found "a Attack Graffiti". For the complimentary font, I wanted a bold but simple font and liked the font "Roboto".

For the key elements of the logo and the brand, the first thing I wanted to add were the splash elements to play off of the word Juse and make it look like the word is being splatted on the wall. The next element, I incorporated was the glitter or stars. I have seen these stars be used with other grafiti like artwork. I added the stars because I thought it would add to the classic grafiti look as well as add a pop to the logo. The next two elements were dripping paint and a brick wall. I included these elements to further the look of grafiti on a wall with the drip aspect of the paint connecting back to the name of Juse.

Here is the final Juse logo. I designed this logo using Adobe Photoshop. From the final logo, I layered the " a Attack Graffiti" font in 3 of the colors from the color scheme. I did this to emphasize the word Juse and it always gave it a 3D effect. I then added the drip around the word Juse and added the whit glitter on to the word to give the word an extra flare. Then I kept it simple with the font of "Supercharged Products" and the underline.

I created these prototypes as image placement holders for their actual product pictures. Since this company is extremely new, they did not have their product pictures ready but they still wanted to get a website up and running. I created these prototypes using Adobe Photoshop.
